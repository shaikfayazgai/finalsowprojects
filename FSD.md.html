Enterprise Role Architecture Before any screen can be specified, developers and QA engineers need to know who is using it and what they are allowed to do\. This section defines the two roles that exist inside the Enterprise Portal, what each role can and cannot see, and what each role can and cannot do\. Every field, every button, and every page in this document is governed by these two roles\. The Enterprise Admin is the primary account holder — they submitted the SOW, they approve the work, they release payments, and they hold all final authority on the platform\. The Reviewer is a team member added by the Enterprise Admin to help with quality validation — they can review evidence and recommend decisions, but they cannot approve payments, manage SOWs, or change account settings\. If you are building a feature and you are unsure who can access it, return to this section first\. ## 2\.1 Role Definitions The Enterprise Portal operates with two roles: the Enterprise Admin \(also called Enterprise Requester\) who is the primary account holder and project owner, and the Reviewer who is assigned to validate contributor deliverables\. The first is SSO — Single Sign\-On — where the user authenticates through their company's own identity provider \(Google Workspace, Microsoft Entra, Okta\) and the platform trusts that result\. The second is email and password, where the platform itself manages credentials\. These two routes produce the same outcome — an active session — but they have different security models, different MFA requirements, and different recovery paths\. One critical rule before reading any of it: there is no role selector on the login page\. The platform always determines the user's role from their identity — the user never tells the system what role they are\. __Method__ __Applies To__ __How It Works__ __SSO — SAML 2\.0 / OIDC__ Enterprise Admin \(primary for organisations with a corporate IdP\)\.
