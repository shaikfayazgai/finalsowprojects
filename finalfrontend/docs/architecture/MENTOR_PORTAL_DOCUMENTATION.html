Section 1

Mentor portal — what it is

GlimmoraTeam is an AI-governed workforce platform: enterprises submit Statements of Work, the platform decomposes them into tasks, matches them to vetted contributors, supervises delivery with AI agents, and routes outcomes through review before payment is released. The Mentor Portal is the platform's irreversibility surface — the human gate between contributor submission and enterprise acceptance, the place where money, credentials, and reputation are decided.

Operator persona

The Mentor

  • Senior reviewer responsible for one or more review pools
  • Handles escalations from reviewers
  • Coaches contributors via watchlist and mentorship hub
  • Owns SLA adherence, governance posture, and AI calibration
  • Distinct from reviewer (handles initial reviews only)

Decision surface

What gets decided here

  • Accept / Reject / Request rework on contributor submissions
  • Resolve escalations (spec ambiguity, quality dispute, conduct)
  • Apply / release governance holds
  • Override AI suggestions and calibrate model
  • Reassign reviewers, rebalance pool capacity
Section 2

Redesign goals (from the strategy doc)

1. Throughput goes up. Composite-priority queue, AI quick-confirm, criterion-anchored feedback.
2. Governance goes deeper. Every decision is signed, audited, and explainable end-to-end.
3. SLA is surfaced, not buried. Three-tier surfacing: hub strip, queue chips, item banner.
4. AI is auditable, not autopilot. Confidence-bounded, source-cited, override-instrumented.
5. One Review Hub, not two surfaces. Mentor and reviewer modes converge into one workspace.
6. Operational hierarchy first. What needs action outranks what's nice to know.

Pre-redesign (V1) state

The previous mentor sidebar grouped routes by activity verb (Dashboard / Reviews / Actions / Settings), which optimized for breadth but blurred operational hierarchy. Reviews, escalations, mentorship, and history were peers — there was no signal that Reviews is the daily-driver surface or that governance & analytics deserve their own zones.

Section 4

Restructured IA (V2)

V2 reorganizes the workspace around operational domains. Six top-level sections, 19 routes total, with Reviews elevated as the primary surface (visually emphasized in the sidebar). Every domain has its own dedicated zone: SLA monitoring lives in Overview, AI gets its own zone, governance is no longer mixed with reviews, and analytics is a peer to operations.

V2 section map

  1. Overview — Operational Dashboard, SLA & Risk Monitor
  2. Reviews primary — Pending, In Progress, Escalated, Governance Holds, Rework
  3. Contributor Insights — Profiles, Submission History, Risk & Reliability
  4. AI Assistance — Suggestions, Flags, Confidence Explanations
  5. Governance — Audit Trail, Policy Violations, Escalation Logs
  6. Analytics — Throughput, SLA Performance, Quality Metrics
  7. Settings — footer section

Hierarchy logic

  • Overview answers "where do I stand?" — read first each day.
  • Reviews is the daily driver — emphasized visually.
  • Contributor Insights is the people layer — context, not action.
  • AI Assistance is auditable; AI is a colleague, not autopilot.
  • Governance is forensics & compliance — separate from live work.
  • Analytics is the meta layer — trend, not state.

Hierarchy rules

  • Section label weight: primary sections render at 9.5px font-bold in brown-700 with a 0.20em tracking, vs 8.5px font-semibold gray-400 / 0.18em for standard.
  • Primary marker: a 6px brand-gradient dot precedes the primary section title.
  • Inter-section separator: a 1px gradient hairline (transparent → 6% black → transparent) sits between titled sections.
  • Vertical rhythm: section margin-top is 10px after the divider, not 18px — divider provides the visual gap.
  • Active state: subtle horizontal brand gradient wash (9% opacity) on the active row + brown-700 text.
  • Collapsed state: section labels disappear; tooltips on icons preserve discoverability.

Section 9.1

KPI Row

Anatomy

  • Two zones, 1.6 : 1 column ratio (action zone larger).
  • Each zone has a section label with a colored rail glyph.
  • Action zone: 3 tiles (SLA Breach Risks, Escalated, Governance Holds), 32px metric.
  • Throughput zone: 3 tiles (Pending, Avg Time, Throughput), 22px metric on grey background.
  • Critical tiles carry an "ACTION" badge with a red ping; warning tiles carry "MONITOR".

Why this works

  • Operator's eye lands on the urgency block first — Fitts-friendly target sizes for tiles needing action.
  • Supporting tiles are visually subordinate, not removed — context is preserved without competing.
  • Severity rail and badge make urgency readable in < 200 ms peripheral glance.
Section 9.2

Priority Review Queue

The dashboard's main worktable. Composite-priority sorted: SLA tier first, then severity, then AI readiness. Critical rows are pinned in a dedicated block above the standard list.

Row affordances

  • 3px severity rail on the row's left edge (breached / critical / warning / watch / healthy).
  • Subtle row tint by tier (red wash on breached/critical) — eliminates row blending.
  • Click row → opens the Review Detail dialog with claim, rework, open-review actions.
  • Hover row → background lifts to white, chevron darkens.
  • Top 3 rows carry numeric priority badges (#1 red-600, #2 red-500, #3 gold-500).

Filter / segment controls

  • Segment chips: All · Breached + Critical · Escalated · AI Ready · Governance Holds
  • The "Breached + Critical" chip turns red when non-empty — urgency telegraphed at the filter level.
  • Search input (250ms debounce, fuzzy across task / contributor / project).
  • "Filters" button opens the power-filter panel (Phase 2: skill, project, enterprise, type, conf, round).
Section 9.3

Governance Alerts Panel

Surfaces four kinds of governance events: Escalation, Blocked workflow, Hold, and Policy warning. Sorted by kind (most operational first), then by severity within kind.

Row anatomy

  • 3px severity rail color-keyed to kind (escalation red, blocked brown, hold gold, policy teal).
  • Kind chip + severity badge (HIGH = filled red, MEDIUM = bordered gold, LOW = neutral).
  • Blocked workflows show a "Workflow halted" badge.
  • Resolution-SLA pill turns bold red under 6h to resolve.
  • Triple-line: title (semibold) → context → meta (raised-by · timestamp · SLA).

Header signaling

  • Caption leads with operational counts: "5 open · 3 high severity · 2 mentor escalations".
  • Escalation filter chip turns red when escalations exist.
  • "View all" routes to /mentor/governance/audit.
Section 9.4

AI Review Insights

AI as an auditable colleague. Three insight kinds: flagged (risk signals like plagiarism), low-confidence (the model is unsure), and recommendation (e.g., quick-confirm candidate, fresh-reviewer suggestion).

Confidence rendering

  • Gauge bar (full-width, 4px tall) tinted by band: forest ≥85, gold ≥65, red <65.
  • Numeric + label: "94% High".
  • Always paired with a one-line summary (the AI's "why").

Trust signals

  • "Auditable" badge in section header — operator knows every AI output is sourced.
  • Each row carries the linked review ID so AI suggestions are traceable to the item.
  • Footer reports saved-time AND calibration % — AI feels integrated into operational load.
Section 9.5

Recent Review Activity

A compressed operational log, not a hero panel. Each entry fits on one ~32px row: timestamp · dot · icon · action label · subject · detail · actor. Filter chips live in the header. The component is intentionally lower visual weight so it sits below action panels without competing.

Governance

  • Escalation Logs: historical escalations, time-to-resolve distribution + breach rate, linked snapshots, resolution rationale, trends by project & pool.
Analytics

Throughput · SLA Performance · Quality
  • Throughput: decisions/day with target band, AI quick-confirm vs full split, bottleneck attribution, capacity vs intake forecast, reviewer leaderboard.
  • SLA Performance: hit-rate timeline by pool/project, breach root-cause, time-to-decision p50/p90/p99, auto-escalation rate by round, recovery time.
  • Quality: outcome mix per reviewer, AI agreement & override calibration, dispute & overturn rate, quality × throughput scatter, coaching opportunities.
Section 11

Section 15

AI explainability & trust contract

Sourced

Every AI score cites the spec section, artifacts, and history it examined. Coverage gaps are surfaced explicitly.

Bounded

Confidence is banded (High ≥85, Medium ≥65, Low <65). No score is taken at face value — band drives the routing.

Instrumented

Overrides are first-class events. Calibration % is shown to the operator continuously, not buried in admin views.
